Syeda Shagufe Hossain

Founder and Executive Director

Shagufe Hossain is the Founder and Executive Director of Leaping Boundaries. Shagufe has completed her undergraduate studies in Administrative Studies with a concentration in Marketing from York University, Canada following which she obtained a Masters in Development Studies from BRAC University, Bangladesh and a Masters in Education and Society with a concentration in Gender Studies from McGill University, Canada. Her thesis focused on how adolescent girls’ in Bangladeshi madrasahs conceptualize their identities as Muslim Bengali women in today’s globalized world. During the course of her graduate studies in both Bangladesh and Canada, Shagufe has concentrated on exploring how individuals conceptualize their own identities to mitigate perceived conflict among multiple dimensions of their identities. Areas of her research interests include identity and identity politics, gender, religion, and peace education, as well as mental health and well-being.

Shagufe has over seven years of professional experience having worked in several sectors of the development industry namely social and economic empowerment, youth development and education at her own organization as well as BRAC, water, sanitation and hygiene at WaterAid, and internships in public policy at Bangladesh Enterprise Institute (BEI) and the International Finance Corporation (IFC). Her work at Leaping Boundaries has focused on two aspects

  • Girls’ empowerment and
  • Prevention of violent extremist ideologies to create peaceful societies.

Shagufe has also worked as a journalist where served as a member of the Editorial team at the Daily Star. She now writes a regular column for the English daily.

As a trailblazer venturing into a very difficult community, Shagufe has been featured on many national dailies including the Daily Star's Generation Hope Issue alongside multiple features on the Daily New Age, the Dhaka Tribune and the Women in Leadership magazine. She was also awarded multiple fellowships, as a Young Connector of the Future under the Swedish Institute, the Dalai Lama Fellowship, the Dutch Visitors Programme Fellowship, membership of the Dhaka Hub of Global Shapers of the World Economic Forum and has been a speaker at TEDXAIUB. She was also awarded the Community Engagement Award of the Department of Integrated Studies in Education at McGill University for her groundbreaking work with madrasahs in Bangladesh.
