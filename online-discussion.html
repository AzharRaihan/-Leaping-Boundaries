Online Discussion on “Creating a safer environment for madrasah students”

Representatives from Madinatul Ulum Mohila Kamil Madrasah, Al Amin Islamia Madrasah and Gawsia Islamia Fazil Madrasah participated in this discussion aimed to review the definitions of harassment and the commitment to creating a safer environment in madrasahs. The main outcomes of the discussion were as follows:

  • # The guidelines which included a definition of sexual harassment and measures to be adopted including public awareness, complaints procedures and punishments, were discussed at length to raise awareness.
  • # Each institution pledged to establish sexual harassment complaint committee compliant with the directives of the High Court. The process of creating properly functioning committees are currently underway.
  • # The participants' needs were gauged and recorded. The institutions expressed that there were significant gaps in training of teachers around ethics and moral values, raising awareness of parents about harassment and security, particularly for female students, building confidence amongst students so they can report cases, and establishing linkages with local police stations.

Youth are selected from public and private universities, trained as facilitators and deployed to madrasahs to act as mentors, mobilizers, para-counsellors and tutors for the madrasah students.

It is a girl focused programme that focuses on increasing visibility of madrasah students on platforms such as the Bangladesh Youth Leadership center's leadership and training programmes, menstrual hygiene programmes such as the Shorno Kishoree on the Channel I, art exhibitions, coding exhibitions and hackathons etc. where they are otherwise underrepresented. While some of these platforms are existing platforms, some are created by the organization in the form of exhibitions, product lines, hackathons with technical and financial support from individual or organizational partners.

In order to effectively represent themselves in these platforms, students undergo a combination of training programmes which builds their knowledge and confidence. The belief of the programme is with increased capacity, a better coping mechanism and an increased visibility, these adolescents will gain greater confidence, a reaffirmed sense of identity and thus will be better able to mitigate any sense of ongoing identity crises.
