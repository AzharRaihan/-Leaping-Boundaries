Leaping Boundaries Foundation aims to uphold the right to education, representations and equality of the most marginalized communities in Bangladesh with a focus on women and girls.

About Us

The primary target group for the programmes consisted of female madrasah students studying at secondary level madrasahs, aged 12-19. The secondary target group consists of youth, male and female under 35, and driven by an ambition to make a positive difference in society and teachers employed at the madrasahs, who receive training to have their capacity built and are also beneficiaries of the organization.

Target Group

Over 1.5 million girls are enrolled in Bangladeshi madrasahs of whom 79% will have drop out by the time they complete high school. While there is no gender segregated data to show how many transition into the workforce, there is an undeniable dearth of advocates for the issues of this highly marginalized population. The stories of female madrasah students in Bangladesh are unheard and unseen, rendering them stigmatized and marginalized. To help youth and adolescents from these sections of society, be an active part of society, embodying positive values that are compatible with national and international values, they need to develop skills and gain access to platforms where they can represent themselves.

The Challenge

The organization employs a two-tiered approach which engages youth at two levels i.e.
  • university students to become changemakers and volunteer trainers through receiving subject specific Training of Trainers (TOT)
  • adolescents and youth in madrasahs who are trained and catered to by university students.
Youth are selected from public and private universities, trained as facilitators and deployed to madrasahs to act as mentors, mobilizers, para-counsellors and tutors for the madrasah students. It is a girl focused programme that focuses on increasing visibility of madrasah students on platforms such as the Bangladesh Youth Leadership Centre's leadership and training programmes, menstrual hygiene programmes such as the Shorno Kishoree on the Channel I, art exhibitions, coding exhibitions and hackathons etc.
where they are otherwise underrepresented. While some of these platforms are existing platforms, some are created by the organization in the form of exhibitions, product lines, hackathons with technical and financial support from individual or organizational partners. In order to effectively represent themselves in these platforms, students undergo a combination of training programmes which builds their knowledge and confidence. The belief of the programme is with increased capacity, a better coping mechanism and an increased visibility, these adolescents will gain greater confidence, a reaffirmed sense of identity and thus will be better able to mitigate any sense of ongoing identity crises.

History

2018

In 2018, Leaping Boundaries Foundation was incorporated as a charitable trust under the Trust Act, 1882.

2016

In 2016, it was incorporated as part of a new organization, Shammo Foundation, registered under the Societies Act 1860 ,started by the founder of the project.

2014

It was redesigned and relaunched in February 2014 with a new goal, a new set of volunteers and two new madrasahs. Because it started as a voluntary project, it operated as a project as part of Garbo Bangladesh Foundation from 2014 to 2016.

2013

The project came to an abrupt halt in 2013 because of a political crisis. It was then that it identified that the challenge faced by the sector was not a lack of English language skills but a lack of platforms where they were able to represent themselves.

2012

It was first initiated in 2012 after the founder, Syeda Shagufe Hossain, read an article on The Daily Star that highlighted the challenges faced by Alia madrasah students. Initially it was a language and leadership development programme that focused on building English Language skills.

Sustainability

Taar Livelihood Scholarship (2020)

A third of the profit generated from Taar, the sustainability initiative of Leaping Boundaries, was set aside in a livelihood scholarship fund. In order to make meaningful livelihood opportunities accessible to the underprivileged madrasah students, a separate scholarship trust fund was set up for them to access so they could avail training, mentorship, seed grants, and pursue higher studies.

Leaping Boundaries Leadership Academy

SPRING accelerator provided support in developing a sustainable revenue model for Leaping Boundaries, through which, an Authentic Leadership Model and a Leadership Academy were created based on the teachings from the Dalai Lama Fellowship and Conscious Leadership. Through SPRING, Leaping Boundaries developed their leadership curriculum and started to venture towards becoming more financially sustainable to ensure that its work with girls could continue. The academy offered training, workshops and boot camps to youths looking to gain hands-on experience in a non-profit organization.

LB Studio

LB Studio is a Communal/ Co-working space, which opened up from January 2019. It offered to host social and creative ventures at a discount. The space was rented out for shaolin kung fu lessons for women, grief circles, yoga lessons, training and workshops. The studio received great attention after it was promoted on the space sharing and renting app called Chhaya.

TAAR

Taar - Connecting Stories was the sustainability initiative of Leaping Boundaries that aimed to make the stories of the female madrasah students known to a wider audience through a product line composed of various gift items. The product design reflected upon their works, words, art and other forms of creative expression to bring to the buyers/audience narratives from the lives of these girls about whom very little is known. This product line served to break stereotypes surrounding madrasah girls and increase their representation and spread their stories, told by them, in mainstream society.

Challenges Faced

Leaping Boundaries faced immense difficulty in expanding partnerships with madrasahs because of a preconceived notion regarding association between Bangladeshi NGOs/NPOs and prominent Christian donors and development organizations. Blatant sexism posed a common barrier as well, with several madrasahs being reluctant to partner with a women-led and women-focused organization. Madrasah authorities oftentimes did not find young volunteers and trainers credible, as they tend to trust older looking trainers. However, there were some madrasahs which were simply unable to find the time to engage students in after-school extracurricular activities.

After reaching out to more than thirty two madrasahs all over Dhaka city throughout the years, Leaping Boundaries ended up successfully partnering with only seven of them, all Alia madrasahs (institutes that unify secular and Islamic education). This demonstrates the overall level of unwillingness and/or inability of this sector to accommodate the activities propositioned by Leaping Boundaries. It is worth noting that this is far more evident in case of Qawmi madrasahs, where representatives from Leaping Boundaries were not even allowed to speak to authorities, let alone interact with their students.

Other Projects

Mug Auction (2015)

Mugs bearing Leaping Boundaries logo were auctioned to raise funds for the activities. BDT 14,900 was raised through multiple individual donations.

Project Scarlet (2016)

Leaping Boundaries and The Left Side Classroom- Donate For Change collaborated with IFMSA Bangladesh to conduct this project for eradicating the stigmas relating to Menstrual Health and Drug Abuse. The project took place in two phases. At first, 25 trainers were selected and trained on "How to provide advocacy on Menstrual Health, Sex Education and Drug Abuse". Then, they went to different schools and madrasahs, including Madinatul Ulum Women’s Kamil Madrasah, to conduct workshops and distribute sanitary napkins.

Laptop Handover to Madinatul Ulum Women's Kamil Madrasah (2016)

Underserved communities like madrasahs lack technological knowledge due to unavailability of opportunities. Leaping Boundaries took the initiative to provide madrasah students with laptops for introducing them with technology education. In 2016, Leaping Boundaries Founder and Project Director, Shagufe Hossain handed over four laptops to Madinatul Ulum Women's Kamil Madrasah for the Collaborative Tech Reasoning (CTR) component that aimed to enhance their knowledge by integrating technology in their education.

Clean Drinking Water (2020)

In a joint collaboration between Leaping Boundaries and Footsteps Bangladesh, water purifiers were provided in three madrasahs around Dhaka city where more than 1700 students will get access to drinking clean water.

Share Your Fare (2020)

In an attempt to cater to the needs of the extended community comprising madrasah students and teachers severely impacted by the lockdown, Leaping Boundaries launched this new initiative in collaboration with Beatnik Digital. The aim of the initiative is to raise funds to provide for rickshaw pullers who safely take madrasah students and teachers from home to their destinations in areas where the partner madrasahs were located. It aimed to ease the financial burden of the lockdown on daily wage earners by distributing a part of the transportation fare that commuters were being able to save due to "work-from-home" conditions being implemented at most workplaces.
